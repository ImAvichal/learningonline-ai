MODULE 1: AI FOUNDATIONS
Tier: Individual Upskill and above
------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
Cut through the hype. Give learners a clear, practical understanding of
what AI actually is, how it works, what it costs, and where value lives.

LESSONS REQUIRED:

Lesson 1.1 — Introduction to AI: What It Is and Isn't
- Define AI clearly: pattern recognition at scale, not magic
- Three categories: Narrow AI (now), General AI (approaching), Agentic AI (emerging)
- The 5 most common AI failure modes in business (with examples)
- Why AI projects fail: expectation vs reality
- The workflow gap concept — where AI value is created
- Real-world examples: customer service, finance, healthcare, professional services

Lesson 1.2 — Types of AI Models and When to Use Each

- Embedding models: semantic search, RAG systems
- Image/vision models: document processing, quality inspection, OCR
- Speech models: transcription, voice-enabled workflows
- Structured action / Agentic AI: multi-step autonomous tasks
- Decision framework: input type → correct model type
- Cost and complexity trade-offs across model types

Lesson 1.3 — Tokens: The Currency of AI
- What is a token: ~4 characters, ~0.75 words
- Input tokens vs output tokens — definition and cost difference
- Real cost formula with worked examples
- Token cost table: GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini, Claude Sonnet, Claude Haiku
- Monthly cost modelling at business scale
- Token optimisation strategies: prompt compression, caching, model right-sizing, batching
- The compounding cost problem at scale

REAL-WORLD PRACTICE SECTION (each lesson):
Must include:
1. Real-world insight from implementation experience
2. Practical example with numbers
3. Why it matters (business impact)
4. Implementation tip
5. "What This Saves You" — concrete cost/risk avoided

Q&A (5 questions per module, scenario-based):
Question themes for Module 1:
- Cost calculation: given tokens × volume × price, calculate monthly cost
- Model selection: given a use case description, pick the right model type
- Hallucination identification: scenario where AI output is confidently wrong

Scoring Categories: Beginner | Developing | Ready to Apply | Advanced

- Implementation tip: one specific, actionable step

- Include at least one pre-filled example row

- Designed for immediate use — not just illustration
